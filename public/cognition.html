Cognition refers to the set of mental processes by which humans acquire knowledge and understanding. It involves several key functions:

Perception

The ability to interpret sensory information.

Perception is the process by which we interpret and make sense of the information our senses (sight, sound, touch, taste, smell) receive from the environment. It helps us understand and interact with the world around us.

Attention

The ability to focus on specific stimuli or thoughts.

Attention is the ability to focus on specific information, tasks, or activities, while ignoring distractions. It involves both sustained focus and the capacity to switch focus as needed.

Memory

The ability to store, retain, and recall information.

Memory involves the processes of storing, retaining, and recalling information. It includes short-term memory (remembering information for a few seconds or minutes) and long-term memory (retaining information over longer periods).

Language

The ability to understand and use language for communication.

Language encompasses the ability to understand and use words and sentences for communication. It involves skills such as vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension.

Reasoning and Problem-Solving

The ability to think logically, make decisions, and solve problems.

This domain involves the ability to think logically, make decisions, and solve problems. It includes understanding concepts, identifying relationships, and applying knowledge to new situations.

Executive Function

Stroop Test

Executive function refers to a set of mental skills that include working memory, flexible thinking, and self-control. These skills help us manage our thoughts, actions, and emotions to achieve goals.
