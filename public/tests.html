Non-Clinical Tests

GHQ-Test

The GHQ-12 (General Health Questionnaire-12) is a screening tool used to assess general mental health and psychological well-being. It consists of 12 questions that focus on recent experiences, typically over the past week.

CFT

Cognition function tests are used to evaluate various aspects of cognitive functioning, including memory, attention, language, problem-solving, and executive functions. These tests can be used in clinical settings to diagnose cognitive impairments or in research settings to study cognitive processes.

AD-8

The AD8, or Ascertain Dementia 8, is a brief screening tool designed to detect very mild dementia. It consists of eight questions and is typically administered to an informant, such as a family member or caregiver, who is familiar with the patient's daily functioning.

PHQ-9

The PHQ-9 aids in the diagnosis and identification of potential depressive symptoms. At follow-up visits, it measures treatment response. The Questionnaire can be clinician or self-administered.

MoCA

The Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA) is a widely used screening tool for detecting cognitive impairment. It assesses various cognitive domains, including attention, memory, language, visuospatial abilities, executive function, and orientation.
